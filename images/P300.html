"He who has succeeded in attaching or detaching his mind to or from the centers [organs of perception] at will has succeeded in Pratyahara, which means, "gathering towards," checking the outgoing powers of the mind, freeing it from the thralldom of the senses. When we can do this, we shall really possess character; then alone we shall have taken a long step towards freedom; before that we are mere machines."

- Swami Vivekananda, "Pratyahara and Dharana" (Beginning raja yoga class, Jan 18, 1896, New York), Raja Yoga, Complete Works: Vol 1
