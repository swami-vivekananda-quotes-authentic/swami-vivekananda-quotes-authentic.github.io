"When I was first learning English, I read an English story book in which there was a story about a dutiful boy who had gone out to work and had given some of his money to his old mother, and this was praised in three or four pages. What was that? No Hindu boy can ever understand the moral of that story. Now I understand it when I hear the Western idea — every man for himself. And some men take everything for themselves, and fathers and mothers and wives and children go to the wall. That should never and nowhere be the ideal of the householder."

- Swami Vivekananda, "The Secret of Work" (Advanced karma yoga class, Dec 20, 1895, New York Vedanta Society), Karma Yoga, Complete Works: Vol 1
